# ⚠️ Where I Disagree / Push Back

## 1. “No need for withdrawal_details table”

You said:

> “description field (500 chars) can hold this data cleanly”

⚠️ This is acceptable for MVP, but has limitations:

### Risks:

* Hard to query/filter (e.g. admin reconciliation)
* No schema validation
* No structured audit trail
* Parsing becomes brittle over time

### Production expectation:

Structured fields are preferred:

* account_name
* bsb
* account_number
* payid

👉 Recommendation:

* MVP: your approach is fine
* Scale/compliance: you will eventually need structured storage

---

## 2. “Existing transactions table replaces ledger_entries”

⚠️ This is the biggest architectural concern.

Your current model:

* Transactions table = single record per action

But in proper accounting systems:

* You need **double-entry ledger**

Why this matters:

* Auditability
* Reconciliation
* Fraud detection
* Regulatory compliance

### Without ledger entries:

You cannot easily prove:

* where funds came from
* where funds went
* internal consistency across wallets

👉 Your current system can work for MVP, but:

* It is not a true accounting system
* It will become a limitation as volume grows

---

## 3. “No admin approval panel needed”

⚠️ This depends on how you operate withdrawals.

If withdrawals are:

* manually reviewed (common early-stage fintech)

Then:

* You **will need an admin/ops interface**

Even a minimal one:

* approve / reject
* view pending withdrawals
* trigger payout execution

Without it:

* withdrawals are either automatic (riskier)
* or partially manual via backend tools

👉 So this is less “not needed” and more:

> “not user-facing, but still operationally required”

---

## 4. Locked balance vs available balance

You said:

> “availableBalance already does this”

⚠️ This is partially correct but conceptually incomplete.

### Correct model:

* available_balance
* locked_balance

Why:

* Prevents race conditions
* Allows:

  * pending withdrawals
  * pending orders
  * reserved funds

👉 Subtracting directly from available balance works, but:

* You lose visibility into reserved vs spent funds
* Harder to debug discrepancies
