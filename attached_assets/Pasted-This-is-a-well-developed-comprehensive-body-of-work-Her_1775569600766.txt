This is a well-developed, comprehensive body of work. Here's an honest assessment:

**What's genuinely strong:**

The regulatory thinking is sound throughout. The fiat/crypto separation in both the database schema and the UI is correctly reasoned — not just aesthetically but legally. The decision to label currency balances as "Remittance & FX regulated" and the wallet as "DCE regulated (AUSTRAC)" directly mirrors how AUSTRAC categorises these activities, which is exactly the right approach. Most early-stage fintechs miss this entirely.

The exchange component redesign is particularly good. The conversion type label — showing dynamically whether it's "Fiat → Fiat", "Fiat → Digital Asset" etc. — is a detail that most platforms don't surface to users, but which regulators immediately look for when assessing whether you understand your own risk exposure.

The minimum viable compliance strategy is also well-calibrated. Adding the `transactions` table and `aml_flags` without demolishing the existing system is exactly how real fintechs stage their compliance buildout — you don't need perfection to register, you need traceability and separation.

**Where I'd push back or add caution:**

The dashboard demo balances are very large — USD $1.9M, GBP £75K, BTC holdings — for a pre-registration platform. If AUSTRAC reviewers see a "demo" account holding what looks like substantial client funds before any licence is granted, it raises questions about whether the platform is already operating. Scale these down significantly for the AUSTRAC submission version.

The "Settlement" field in the exchange component is still incomplete across the full conversation — it was flagged but never fully resolved. Settlement timeframe disclosure is a specific ATO and AUSTRAC expectation for remittance providers. It needs a concrete value: T+0, T+1, or "up to 2 business days" — not a blank or placeholder.

The ABN shown in the DCE disclaimer (54 690 827 608) needs to be verified as active and correctly linked to the registered entity before this goes live. Displaying an ABN on a financial services platform that doesn't match ASIC records creates an immediate compliance problem.

**Overall verdict:**

For a pre-registration, pre-launch platform, this is well above average. The architecture thinking, the UI separation, the disclosure language, and the staged compliance approach are all defensible. The remaining gaps — demo balance sizing, settlement disclosure, ABN verification — are fixable in a day. This is 85% ready for AUSTRAC submission.
