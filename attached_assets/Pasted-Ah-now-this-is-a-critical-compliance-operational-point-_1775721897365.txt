You cannot safely use an email-based internal transfer system like “send to recipient@amax.com
” if AMAX is not a custodian of the recipient’s wallet. Here’s why:

1️⃣ Why an Email-Based Internal Transfer Doesn’t Work Without Custody
Internal ledger requires control over assets.
If AMAX doesn’t hold the recipient’s ETH or USDT (i.e., non-custodial), then “sending” ETH to another user by email is just a promise, not an actual transfer.
No blockchain settlement.
Without custody, there’s no ledger balance to decrement/increment, so internal transfers are not enforceable.
Regulatory risk.
AUSTRAC expects accurate audit trails for all transfers. Using emails without custody breaks traceability — a high-risk compliance gap.
2️⃣ Feasible Alternatives
Option	Feasible?	Notes
Internal Transfer by Email (Non-Custodial)	❌	Cannot move crypto that AMAX doesn’t control. Only a record/promise, not real settlement.
Internal Ledger (Custodial Wallets)	✅	If AMAX holds ETH/USDT in its wallets, internal transfers are instant and traceable.
Blockchain Transfer (External)	✅	Non-custodial, compliant if you collect Travel Rule info. Fees apply.
Layer 2 / Smart Contract Escrow	✅ (Advanced)	Could allow near-instant transfers using smart contracts, but AMAX needs to manage escrow or use a protocol.
3️⃣ Recommendation

If AMAX is non-custodial, then you cannot have “internal transfers” by email. The options you have:

Remove Option 2 entirely, and just have:
External wallet withdrawal
Convert-to-AUD + bank withdrawal
Or move to a custodial model:
AMAX manages hot/cold wallets for users
Internal ledger transfers become feasible and auditable
Optional workaround (pseudo-internal transfer):
Use smart-contract-based ledger on-chain
“Internal transfer” = moving tokens within a smart contract account (requires development, gas fees)

💡 Summary:

“Internal Transfer via email” is not feasible without AMAX being a custodian. For non-custodial users, only external blockchain transfers with Travel Rule compliance are possible.
