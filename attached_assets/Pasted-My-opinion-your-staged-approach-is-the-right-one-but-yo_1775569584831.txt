My opinion: **your staged approach is the right one, but you should be stricter about what is “good enough” now versus what is only for later.**

What I agree with most is this:

* **Do not rebuild everything now**
* **Keep the current shell**
* **Separate fiat and crypto clearly in UI and data handling**
* **Use the existing transaction model as the phase-1 audit trail**
* **Add a small AML/review layer**
* **Use honest pre-launch wording on the website**
* **Treat portfolio totals as reporting, not operational truth** 

That is the practical path.

Where I would be firmer is this:

**1. Don’t say “pass AUSTRAC” too confidently**
I would say “more defensible for AUSTRAC registration” rather than guaranteed approval. Approval will also depend on your AML/CTF program, KYC process, business narrative, responsible managers, website wording, and consistency across the application. 

**2. The UI is already close, but wording matters a lot**
The layout direction is strong: one dashboard, two clearly separated systems, optional combined value. That is the right design. But labels must stay careful:

* use **“Total Portfolio Value (AUD equivalent)”**
* say fiat and crypto are **held separately**
* avoid anything that makes crypto look like cash in an ordinary money account
* clearly label demo environments as demo only 

**3. Your minimum-change backend should upgrade what exists, not invent a new story**
Because your app already has wallets and transactions, the best move is not a dramatic redesign. It is:

* strengthen transaction metadata
* add review/risk fields
* add simple AML flags
* enforce wallet type separation
* keep portfolio as downstream display only

That is better than pretending you already need full ledger architecture tomorrow.

**4. The dropdown issue is real**
This is not a fatal compliance failure, but it is exactly the kind of subtle product issue that creates ambiguity. My view is:

* yes, users can access both fiat and crypto in one exchange area
* no, they should not appear as one undifferentiated list
* group them by asset type or use tabs/toggles first

That small UI fix does a lot of compliance work. 

So overall:

**Your strategy is correct. Your current app should be evolved, not rebuilt.**
For phase 1, I would aim for:

* honest website/disclosures
* separate fiat and crypto presentation
* strengthened transaction traceability
* basic AML review workflow
* no misleading “registered/live” claims unless already true

That is my real opinion: **good commercial judgment, good regulatory direction, but keep the claims conservative and the separation explicit.**

The biggest mistake now would be overbuilding the backend or overstating regulatory readiness. The best move is a clean, believable phase-1 compliance build.
