Here's the full breakdown:

---

**The core problem with the non-custodian framing**

Whether you use Coinbase or any other external custodian, AMAX is still acting as an intermediary that:
- takes fiat from users
- routes it to a custodian
- shows users a representative balance

Under the brand new Corporations Amendment (Digital Assets Framework) Bill 2025 (passed 1 April 2026 — 8 days ago), the law defines a Digital Asset Platform (DAP) as an intermediary-operated facility that possesses clients' digital tokens and records client interests in an account — inclusive of exchanges, brokers, custodians and some wallet providers.

AMAX fits that definition regardless of who the backend custodian is. The routing through Coinbase doesn't remove AMAX from the custody chain in ASIC's eyes — it just adds a layer to it.

---

**What your AUSTRAC DCE registration actually covers**

Your DCE registration covers the exchange function only — converting fiat to crypto and back. It does not cover:

- Acting as a DAP (recording client interests in an account)
- Holding or arranging custody of digital assets on behalf of users
- Remittance services (that's a separate AUSTRAC registration category — Remittance Sector Provider)

These are three distinct regulatory boxes. A single DCE registration does not tick all three.

---

**Remittance specifically — a separate issue**

If AMAX is also moving money on behalf of clients (e.g. fiat to crypto cross-border, or crypto transfers between users), that is remittance under the AML/CTF Act and requires a separate AUSTRAC Remittance Sector registration. DCE ≠ remittance. They can coexist but must be registered independently.

---

**So what should AMAX actually do?**

Here's the honest regulatory map given the new law:

| Function | Registration / Licence Required |
|---|---|
| Fiat ↔ crypto exchange | AUSTRAC DCE ✅ (you have this) |
| Showing user balances / acting as DAP | AFSL required under new Digital Assets Framework — must apply |
| Remittance (moving value cross-border or between users) | AUSTRAC Remittance Sector registration — separate |
| Custody (holding crypto on user behalf) | AFSL + custody authorisation — or outsource to licensed custodian and disclose properly |

---

**The correct structure for AMAX if you want to operate cleanly**

The cleanest model that keeps AMAX as a DCE-only entity without triggering AFSL obligations is:

1. AMAX executes the exchange (DCE function — covered)
2. An external licensed custodian (Independent Reserve, or a future AFSL-holding entity) holds the assets in the user's name — not AMAX's name
3. AMAX shows a read-only balance pulled from the custodian's API — not its own ledger
4. AMAX never touches the crypto — it only touches the fiat side of the transaction
5. The user has a direct contractual relationship with the custodian for custody

Under this model, AMAX's non-custodian claim is true and defensible. Under the current model where AMAX maintains its own internal ledger of crypto balances, it is not.
