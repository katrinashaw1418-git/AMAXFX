What the new law actually targets

A DAP is a facility where an operator possesses clients' digital tokens and records client interests in an account — this captures exchanges, brokers, custodians and some wallet providers.

The trigger word is "possesses". The law targets entities that hold crypto. Not entities that arrange trades.

The three exit doors from AFSL

Low-value DAP exemption: an issuer of a DAP does not need to hold an AFSL if the total market value of transactions across all its platforms does not exceed $10 million over a 12-month period.

Incidental arranging relief: a person primarily engaged in a non-financial services business is not required to hold an AFSL only because, in the ordinary course of that business, they arrange for a person to use a DAP or TCP — the relief is limited to introductions and incidental arranging.
