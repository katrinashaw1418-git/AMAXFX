1️⃣ Key Regulatory Principle

For AUSTRAC and FATF compliance, there are two different models:

Custodial Exchange / VASP:
AMAX holds user crypto balances in its wallets.
Can facilitate instant internal transfers, fiat-to-crypto trades, and ledger-based operations.
Fully auditable; compliant if KYC/AML/FATF Travel Rule is enforced.
Non-Custodial Exchange / Broker:
AMAX never holds the crypto.
Users buy crypto, but it’s delivered directly to their own wallet (self-custody).
AMAX only facilitates the transaction, like an introducer or broker.

Since you said AMAX is not a custodian, your platform falls into model 2.

2️⃣ Can a “Fiat → Crypto” page be compliant if non-custodial?

Yes — with conditions:

Required Compliance Measures:
KYC Verification (Customer Identity)
Must verify the user before the fiat purchase.
Collect full legal name, address, DOB, and proof of identity.
AML / Source of Funds Checks
Monitor large or suspicious purchases.
Ask for the source of funds for high-value transactions (salary, investment, etc.)
Crypto Delivery / Destination Wallet
Since AMAX doesn’t hold crypto:
Must require a user-supplied wallet address for delivery.
Validate that the address is compatible with the purchased asset/network.
Collect Travel Rule info if the transaction involves another VASP.
Optional: limit high-value transfers to self-hosted wallets vs. VASP wallets.
Travel Rule / Reporting
If a cross-VASP transaction occurs (e.g., user sends purchased crypto to Binance/Coinbase):
AMAX must collect originator info.
If delivery is to self-custody, Travel Rule may not apply immediately, but audit logs of the purchase must exist.
Transaction Monitoring
Keep records of every purchase.
Flag unusual volumes or patterns.
Must be auditable for AUSTRAC.
Risk Disclosure
Users must know AMAX is non-custodial.
Funds are delivered directly to their wallet, not held on platform.

A fiat-to-crypto exchange page can be compliant for a non-custodial AMAX, as long as:
