✅ My view (aligned with your approach)
✔️ You SHOULD separate:
Fiat balances (e.g. AUD, USD)
Digital assets (crypto wallet)

Because from a regulatory lens (including AUSTRAC):

They are different regulated activities
They carry different risks (custody, volatility, AML exposure)
They may sit in different legal structures / safeguarding arrangements

👉 So yes — never merge them into a single “balance”

🧠 Where I’d refine your design (important)
⚠️ Don’t fully “integrate” them
⚠️ Don’t fully “isolate” them either

👉 The winning model is:

“Unified dashboard, segregated balances, optional consolidated view”

🧭 Recommended dashboard structure (best practice)
🔝 Top layer (portfolio summary — optional but powerful)
“Total Portfolio Value (AUD equivalent)”

Small disclaimer:

“Includes fiat and digital assets. Digital assets are subject to market volatility.”

👉 This gives users a holistic view
BUT doesn’t legally mix balances

🟦 Section 1: Currency Balances (Fiat)
AUD, USD, etc.

Clear label:

“Held in fiat accounts”

Show:
Available balance
Pending (if applicable)
🟨 Section 2: Digital Wallet (Crypto)
BTC, ETH, stablecoins

Label:

“Digital assets held in wallet”

Show:
Quantity (e.g. 0.5 BTC)
AUD equivalent
🔄 Between sections (important UX)

Add clear actions:

“Convert (FX)”
“Buy/Sell Crypto”
“Transfer”

👉 This shows controlled interaction between systems, not blending

📊 Transaction history (combined but filterable)
Unified feed:
Fiat + crypto transactions
Filter:
All / Fiat / Crypto

👉 This is what users expect (and auditors like traceability)

⚠️ Critical compliance nuance (most people miss this)

Even in UI:

👉 You must not imply that crypto = money held like fiat

So avoid:

❌ “Total Balance: $50,000” (if includes crypto without clarity)
❌ Mixing assets in one wallet visually

Instead:

✅ “Portfolio Value”
✅ Separate sections with clear labels
🧾 Why your approach is correct (from regulator + bank lens)

Your logic aligns with how:

Regulators think → segregation of funds
Banks think → risk isolation
Auditors think → clear traceability
